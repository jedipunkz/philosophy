New Haven, CT, Yale University, Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library, Marston MS 164

Description: ff. ii - 101: - Fifteenth century, Spain. Endleaves and pastedowns sewn with book. On rear pastedown an
undeciphered Hebrew (?) inscription. Sewn on four double, twisted, tawed skin supports laced into grooves in wooden
boards and wedged. The spine is lined with coarse cloth between supports (Moorish influence). A strip of parchment extends
inside the boards under the pastedowns. Green and gold, five core (?) endbands are sewn on tawed skin cores laced into
the boards and pegged. Covered in blue tawed skin with two fastenings, the catches on the lower board. - Purchased from
Enzo Ferrajoli through Nicolas Rauch of Geneva in 1958 by L. C. Witten, who sold it the same year to Thomas E. Marston. -
Shailor, B. Catalogue of Medieval and Renaissance Manuscripts in the Beinecke Rare Book and Manuscript Library, Marston
MS 164.
