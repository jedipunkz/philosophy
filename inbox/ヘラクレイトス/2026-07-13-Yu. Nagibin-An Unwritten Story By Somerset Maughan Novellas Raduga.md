Although collections of my short stories
have appeared in English-speaking countries at
various times, it would be presumptuous of
me to assume that my name is well-known to
a wide foreign reading public. So let me first
introduce myself.

I was born into an office worker’s family in
Moscow in 1920. We lived in a fine old part
of Moscow near Chistoprudni Boulevard,
which I have devoted not a few short stories
to, and our house was surrounded by old
churches, mansions, and gardens planted in
the seventeenth century. Close-by was the
golden spire of the famous Menshikov tower,
which was then the highest in Moscow. My
peers and I learnt about Russian history not
from books but from Moscow buildings,
churches, gardens and boulevards. My impres-
sions of those far-off days resulted in my
Book of Childhood which has been translated

As a teenager I had no clearly-defined,
obvious talents, and upon leaving school,
unlike most of my comrades, I did not know
what to do next. Both my mother and my
stepfather, the writer Y. Rikachev, hoped
that I would gain eminence as an engineer

To keep them happy I bought some test

while. And then one day there was an explo-

To make up for it, I felt more and more at
