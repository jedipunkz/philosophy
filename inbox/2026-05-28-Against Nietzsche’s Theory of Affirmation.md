## Abstract

This paper presents affirmation as the central normative category of NietzscheвҖҷs positive ethics. The paper argues in particular for two interpretive claims: first, that from Beyond Good and Evil onwards, we find a new variety of Nietzschean affirmation (вҖҳnatural affirmationвҖҷ), which is crucial to the strategy of his later works; and second, for reasons internal to his own philosophical aims, NietzscheвҖҷs new variety of affirmation is seriously flawed. The author argues for the second claim on the basis that Nietzsche himself requires that affirmation not perform exactly the role he requires that it perform. The author contends that we are thus faced with a major challenge to NietzscheвҖҷs late philosophical projectвҖ”that (late) Nietzschean affirmation fails by NietzscheвҖҷs own standards.
