PREFACE

By now the triennial Symposia Aristotelica need no general
introduction to scholars. The third, whose Proceedings appear
in this volume, was held, like the first, at Oxford, but this was
something of an accident; it remains the intention of the Sym-
posiasts to gather each time at a different centre of Aristotelian
studies in Europe.

The third Symposium marked an important and extremely
fruitful departure from previous practice. It was decided that
the discussions should be concentrated on a single work of
Aristotle, and in particular on a work rich in debatable material
but relatively poor in commentaries. The Topics, with its natural
consort the De Sophisticis Elenchis, was an obvious choice, and all
the papers in the present volume concern themselves wholly or
chiefly with Aristotle’s textbook of dialectic. The place and the
value of Aristotle’s dialectic in his philosophical and scientific
work have seized the attention of scholars increasingly in recent
years, and a joint scrutiny of the source-book was evidently due.
Most of the major problems associated with the interpretation of
the work will be found raised in the following pages, and some
of them, I am inclined to think, will be found solved.

Three of the original papers, those of Professors von Fritz,
Furley and Plezia, were withdrawn ; Professor von Fritz’s paper
has appeared, much enlarged, as a monograph on éraywyi.
Two of the papers printed here, those of Professor de Vogel
and Fraulein Soreth, were presented in the lamented absence
of their authors; they therefore appear without the benefit of
those second thoughts, encouraging or daunting, which parti-
cipants hope to carry away from the fray.The papers of Professor
Ryle and Father de Pater can be read as supplements to the
books they have recently published in this field.

It remains to thank the Trustees of the Jowett Fund, as once
before at the first Symposium, for the generous assistance which
made it possible to copy and circulate the papers in advance of

the meeting.
G. E. L. O.
