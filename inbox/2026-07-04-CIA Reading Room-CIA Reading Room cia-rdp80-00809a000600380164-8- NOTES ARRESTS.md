SIK-BOLGAR WING LIQUIDATED -- Munich, Hungaria, 19 Jan 51

Endre Sik, former envoy to the US and leader of the political department
of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, was arrested during the recent purges. It
is rumored that Elek Bolgar, envoy to Britain, will also be recalled soon.

Sik and Bolgar have been active Communists since 1939, occupied professorial
chairs in the USSR, nnd adhere strongly to the principles of ideal Communism.
Neither exploited his official position for personal gain.

Others hela at State Security Authority headquarters are Gyorgy Boloni,
envoy to Holland, who has not been permitted to return to his post for months,
Endre Havas, secretary, observer, and agent of Mihaly Karolyi at Paris, Gyorgy
Kaldor, one-time foreign political writer for the Pester Lloyd, who turned
from Socialism to Communism. ‘These have been arrested to forestall attempts

to turn to Titoism.

Ivan Boldizsar, previously Peasant Party, and later, "extra-party” state
secretary for the press, and who is on good terms with several persons who
have been arrested, is showing signs of increasing anxiety. Geza Losonczy,
State Secretary under Revai, is in a similar situation. He has been unusually
quiet and has retired to the background.

The drive on intellectual elements of the party has substantially shaken
Gyorgy Lukacs, and Bela Fogarasi, the chief bolseviser of the universities.

In internal politics, the Warricss for Liberty Association has been cen--
sw. 1 for tolerating spies in its ranks and for lacking vigilance. Zoltan
Donko, secretary of the Pioneers, was obliged to print an article in the Sza-
bad Nep severely criticizing himself and the Pioneers. In such instances pub-
lic action is only the aftermath of previous purges.

DR ISTVAN RIESZ DEAD -- Rome, Bolletino Danubiano, 3 Nov 50

It is reported that Dr Istvan Riesz died in prison suddenly. Dr Riesz
was arrested by the political police a few months ago and was subsequently
transferred to the Vac penitentiary. It is rumored that he was slain in the
course of an interrogation, although other reports attribute his death to a
stroke.

Dr Riesz is remembered for his servility to the Communist regime in Hun-
gary during his 5-year' tenure 4s Minister of Justice. He was responsible for
the new Hungarian penal code which cost hundreds of thousands of Hungarians
life, liberty, family, and property. He was also an ardent advocate for the
incorporation of the Social Democratic Party into the Communist Party, and for
the persecution of his former comrades. He accumulated an enormous fortune
py accepting heavy bribes for the release of political prisoners.
