Dialectical Renditions of Artistic Life - Idealism as a Modernism

By Shomit Sirohi

Introduction

Idealism as a Modernism - covers Ulysses and such subjects on daily life - that we are even surrealists with
Breton, and all of this then is in standard Sirohianism which covers and admires Hegel’s job at an art gallery -
through Sirohian lens - that in fact one has to bring that modernism or surrealism - or life crisis - one has to
bring it alive. Something unique has to be said, something absolute has to happen, - as Badiou argues - even
in the order of daily life, events must happen. Which Cornel West calls the judgement each day - that infinite
judgement becoming infinite infinite judgement - in a way too idealist Sirohi remains actually, the dollar goes
to Hegel.

I. The Issue of Stress, or Delusion, - the problem becomes its own solution

In fact the problem of the average Three Penny Opera, that in fact average job lives, even theatre lives, is full
of average problems - basically a problem on health, or stress, or delusion or even a profound crisis, a full
profound madness - all of this is the average problem of a Three Penny Opera - what it means in dialectics, is
the problem of life, and all its dilemmas, called by dialectics - dilemmatic, or aporias, becoming contradiction
- why Proust lacks the negativity of problems - Three Penny then is that dilemma of life, and crises in general
average ways becoming in fact a contradiction - which then means dialectics is about living problems, living
contradictions and crises - more than economics or politics - the lifeblood of the dialectic is vacillation.

II. Thesis ant-thesis and then synthesis like a photograph

In fact the thesis is a profound man in a photograph, the anti-thesis is disorderly even bad lives which is
captured in a photograph as profundity and profound problems of life. Of course Wittgenstein must be read
with life and its crises. A Profound language game, whose truth is simple language games. That in fact the
dialectic vacillates again that - in fact the objective movement of history, revolt and the masses then is
necessary, but in a certain subjective way it is also the problem of intellectual life with its crisis as perennial
daily problems, from the wage to the publishing to the academics to life as unemployment and in fact another
crisis - the waves of life then are always dialectical solutions throughout life - as in fact that synthesis I call
paranthesis.

III. Hegel and Sirohi - Again the differences - the Master and that young Master who then is in fact
Young

In fact the young answer always solves the problem - the crisis is bourgeois, the life is bourgeois. The
problems are bourgeois, and we are bourgeois and so live the absolute - as some type of difference and
repetition.
