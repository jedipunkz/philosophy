CHAPTER VI

THE LADDER—A CLEW?

No crime was ever committed without a clew.

That is the edict, from time immemorial, to which
every criminologist subscribes. No matter how clever,
no matter how carefully planned, no matter how ex¬
traordinary the intelligence of the criminal—in short,
