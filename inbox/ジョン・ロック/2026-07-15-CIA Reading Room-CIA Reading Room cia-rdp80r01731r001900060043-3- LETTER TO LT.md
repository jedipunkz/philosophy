Dear General Walters:

It was certainly good to meet you at Herr-Doctor
Fritz Kraemer's bfrthday party the other evening
at Ft. McNair and to see your sister again. I've
heard so much about you from Joe and Augusta Petrone,

You may find it useful to skim the attached Final

Report of our ''success story'! in supporting ROTC

during the crisis years when the Peace Movement --
sometimes in concert with SDS, Young Trotskyites and
Maoists -- sought to drive ROTC off the civilian campus.

This may be a classical case history of what can be
achieved by the initiativeof private philanthropy
working in "partnership'' with universities and pow~

ful government entities -- in this case, the Department
of Defense and all Branches of the Armed Forces.

The mission was to reverse the radical trend on the
campus and provide an intellectual rationale for ade-
quate National Defense.

During the five years of the ROTC Enrichment Program,
we commissioned 233 civilian professors to give 1,530
lectures to ROTC Cadets on 201 campuses.

In addition, we trained 888 Professors of Military/
Aerospace Science in politico-military affairs, geo-
economics and grand strategy.
