## Abstract

<p>This chapter explains that while Division I of <italic>Being and Time</italic> presents a detailed interpretation of Dasein, it stops short of exploring authentic existence, which Division II addresses. Martin Heidegger investigates phenomena tied to authenticity, such as death, conscience, and resoluteness, to illuminate Dasein's temporality and reinterpret everydayness. The chapter also mentions Division III, which although never completed was intended to clarify what it means to be and to deepen the analysis of Being itself through the lens of time as its horizon. Heidegger uses terms like horizonal schema, ecstases, and praesens to describe how temporal structure makes understanding Being possible. However, many fundamental questions remain unresolved, and Heidegger ultimately abandoned this path, marking a turning point in his thinking.</p>
