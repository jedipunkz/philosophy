COURSE OBJECTIVES

CS Records II (Biographic Research) is intended
for those Officers who support operations through
recovery of information about persons of interest
to the Clandestine Services. It deals with the
principles, techniques, and specific procedures used
in exploiting the various records of the Agency and
other sources of biographic information, primarily
at Headquarters.

Biographic Research has often been called "name
checking" and regarded as no more than an administra-

tive process. For this reason, people with insufficient

knowledge of either the available sources of informa-
tion or of the appropriate extent of the investigation
are sometimes assigned to the task. As the Agency's
operations have become more complex and the records
derived from them have become more extensive, more
highly specialized knowledge has become necessary

in order to perform adequate name-checks. This

course provides an introduction to the skills needed
in biographic research, and represents a synthesis

of the experience of professionals in the field.

Students are instructed in CS Requirements for
biographic research, its place in the overall investi-
gative process, and in the available resources and
repositories of information. Exercises in research
and in preparation of the necessary reports form the
bulk of the course, together with a practical exercise

in the Case Officer's responsibilities to the CS Records

System at the conclusion of his work.
